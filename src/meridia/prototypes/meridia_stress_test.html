Thesis for Stress Testing
What Meridia Is
A FinTech intelligence layer built on a BaaS model — like Relay on Thread Bank, Mercury on Evolve. No charter required. Meridia owns the intelligence and experience layer between consumers and chartered institutions.
The Bilateral Model
Every platform reads one direction. Meridia reads both — producing a guided roadmap for the individual AND a qualified pipeline signal for the institution simultaneously from the same intake.
The JPMC Angle
Chase (retail/CRA) and JP Morgan (institutional) have no designed bridge. Chase gets CRA-documented customers. JP Morgan gets visible pipeline approaching qualification. Neither changes its positioning.
The Structural Position
The Rockefeller refinery — not owning the oil, owning the layer everything passes through. Meridia becomes infrastructure that banks depend on, not a product they compete with.
The Moat
The corpus. Behavioral + financial intelligence from both sides, accumulating simultaneously. Cannot be purchased or replicated. Only exists because Meridia builds intake, guidance, and institutional translation from the same infrastructure.
Current State — Honest
Sophisticated referral engine. Working consumer intake. FastAPI backend on home NAS. No chartered bank partner. No signed institutional agreements. No BaaS integration yet. BaaS layer transforms this from referral engine to infrastructure position.
The Question This Must Survive
Why can't Chase build this internally? Why can't a well-capitalized FinTech replicate it? What prevents JPMC from acquiring the referral engine before BaaS is established?
