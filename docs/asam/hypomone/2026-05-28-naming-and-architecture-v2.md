### Why each name

- **Eden** — original entrustment. The first place. The garden where humans were given capacity and asked to steward it (Genesis 2:15). Already the parent entity name; the meaning was always there.
- **Meridia** — from *meridian*: the navigational line, and the sun's apex. The line you follow to the highest point. Already established as the operating LLC.
- **Hypomone** — from Greek *ὑπομονή*: patient endurance, abiding-under, staying through. Distinctively clean in the US lending/members'-club lane (verified). Carries the aged-spirit metaphor in its meaning — patience that produces character over time. The posture the rest of the industry structurally cannot offer.

## The Solera Principle

A *solera* is the tiered, fractional aging-and-blending system used for cognac, sherry, and aged spirits. Young liquid enters the top tier; over years it moves down through the *criaderas*; the bottom row — the solera proper — is never fully emptied. A fraction is drawn off and refilled from the tier above. The founding stock is never fully drawn down; the young is elevated by the seasoned and the seasoned kept alive by the young; consistency at origin compounds into depth over time.

That is the cognac arc rendered as an *operating principle* rather than a metaphor — and so it gets named as such. **The Charter is what a member signs; The Solera Principle is what they join.** One names the instrument; the other names the system the instrument enrolls them into. They are different acts and they take different names.

**Open architectural question.** *Solera* as named here can operate at two levels, and the institution should eventually be clear which it intends:

- *Metaphorical* — solera is the image of how the membership ages; the language used in Charter prose and ritual; not literally implemented in bylaws.
- *Mechanical* — the institution borrows the solera structure literally: the founding cohort's seats are preserved by design in perpetuity; new cohorts enter in defined tiers; specific participation rights, governance weight, or benefit flow move down through the tiers the way liquid does. That would be a real piece of architecture with consequences for membership rights, cohort admission, and the founding generation's preserved role over decades.

Both versions are legitimate; they are different institutions. The decision belongs to the founder's design work as the operating bylaws and membership rules are drafted. Worth flagging that a sharp investor will eventually ask: *"What does solera mean structurally, not poetically?"* — so an answer (or a deliberately-held question) should be ready when the conversation matures.

## The founding principles (interior)

Printed on the wall, named in The Charter, taught at induction. Not consumer marketing copy — the grammar by which the institution governs itself.

- **Zakhar** (Hebrew, זָכַר) — *to remember*, in the active sense: recall and act. Past brought forward. Lineage made operational. *Why the institution exists.*
- **Shema** (Hebrew, שָׁמַע) — *to hear*, in the active sense: listen and respond. Each member received before being measured. *What the institution does in every encounter.*
- **Hupomonē** (Greek, ὑπομονή) — *to abide-under*. Endurance born of abiding. *How the institution stays true across years.*

## Her organs (interior)

What she *does* and what she *offers*. Live in the architecture, not on the door.

- **Aletheia** (Greek, ἀλήθεια) — *un-concealment*. What she does to her members: brings into the open the genuine capacity the conventional system has hidden. The cure to the federally-documented DTI denial mechanism, in one word.
- **Aegis** (Greek, αἰγίς) — *the shield*. What she offers her members: protection, sponsorship, covering. To be a member is to be *under her aegis*.

- The institution is referred to as *she* / *her* in voice, ceremony, and charter language — the way ships, old houses, and storied institutions have always been spoken of.
- Members are **the reserved** — held in trust, set apart, kept for.

## What this is *not*

- Not "HER Capital" or "HER" + finance noun — that lane is saturated and at least one entity holds a registered trademark in it.
- Not branded as the captive insurance arm, the trust apex, or any future vehicle — those live *inside* this architecture as future tiers, not as the public name.
- Not a research project or a beta. The founding cohort signs The Charter as charter members; they are not test users.
- Not "Solera," "Aletheia," "Aegis," "Cornerstone Capital," "Bedrock," "Auburn," "Resurgens," "Gate City," "Terminus," or "Sabr" as the institution's name — every one of those is encumbered in the launch lane; each is documented and out.
