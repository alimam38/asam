# Claude Design: Anthropic's New AI-Powered Visual Workspace

## TL;DR
- **Claude Design** is a research-preview product from Anthropic Labs, launched **April 17, 2026**, that lets users create polished visual work — prototypes, slide decks, one-pagers, wireframes, marketing collateral, and code-powered interactive experiences — by talking to Claude on a chat-plus-canvas surface at **claude.ai/design**, powered by the new **Claude Opus 4.7** vision model.
- It is bundled (no separate SKU) for **Claude Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise** subscribers, with its own **separate weekly usage allowance** metered independently from chat and Claude Code; extra usage bills at standard API rates. It is **off by default for Enterprise** and must be enabled by an admin.
- Its most distinctive feature is a **one-click handoff bundle to Claude Code** that preserves design system, components, and intent — turning prototype-to-production into a closed loop within Anthropic's stack. Early customer reports are strong (Brilliant: 20+ prompts in other tools collapsed to ~2; Datadog: a week of design back-and-forth into one conversation), but practitioners and Hacker News commenters consistently flag heavy token burn against weekly quotas, generic-feeling output without a properly extracted design system, and rough edges typical of a research preview.

## Key Findings

**What it is.** Claude Design is the second major product to ship out of Anthropic Labs (after Claude Cowork) and Anthropic's most direct expansion beyond foundation models into the application layer historically held by Figma, Adobe, and Canva. Its interface is split: a chat panel on the left, a live visual canvas on the right. You describe what you want; Claude produces a first version; you refine through four channels — typed conversation, inline comments dropped on specific elements, direct text/element editing on the canvas, and a "Tweaks" panel of custom sliders that Claude generates contextually (spacing, color temperature, typography scale, density, etc.). Output is real code under the hood (HTML/CSS/JS, sometimes React-like components), which is why prototypes can include voice, video, shaders, 3D, and built-in AI calls — what Anthropic calls "frontier design."

**Who it's for.** Anthropic positions it for two audiences simultaneously: experienced designers who normally have to "ration exploration" and want to try a dozen directions quickly, and non-designers (founders, PMs, marketers, account executives, sales) who want to turn an idea into something visible without learning Figma. The product also explicitly serves the design-to-engineering handoff for product teams already using Claude Code.

**Model and timing.** It is powered by **Claude Opus 4.7**, which Anthropic shipped one day earlier on April 16, 2026. Opus 4.7's vision upgrade — from a 1,568-pixel/1.15-megapixel ceiling to **2,576 pixels / ~3.75 megapixels** on the long edge — is what makes a credible design-generation product plausible from a foundation-model company; it also brings improvements in software engineering (SWE-bench Verified jumped from 80.8% to 87.6% per third-party trackers), instruction following, and a new "xhigh" effort tier. API pricing is unchanged at $5/M input and $25/M output tokens, but a new tokenizer can use 1× to 1.35× the tokens of prior models on the same text — a quiet cost factor that matters for Claude Design users whose interactive output is token-heavy.

**Availability.** Research preview, rolling out gradually to Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise subscribers throughout April 17, 2026. Web-only at launch (no desktop app, no terminal integration). Free-tier users do not get access. Enterprise organizations must explicitly turn it on in Organization settings → Capabilities; admins can also gate it through custom roles in a phased rollout.

**Relationship to Claude Code.** This is the strategic core of the product. When a design is ready, the Export menu offers a "Handoff to Claude Code" option that packages the design files, chat history, design system, component decisions, and architectural intent into a structured bundle — sent either to Claude Code running locally or to Claude Code Web at claude.ai/code. Claude Code receives the bundle as context and implements the feature, rather than reinterpreting a static mockup. Multiple practitioners (e.g., Austin Lau's launch-day workflow thread, Builder.io's review, Brilliant's testimonial) describe this as the missing piece that closes the design-to-production loop in a way Figma → frontend dev, v0, Lovable, and Bolt do not. The two products are explicitly complementary, not overlapping: Claude Design produces front-end visual artifacts; Claude Code handles agentic coding inside an actual codebase.

**Design capabilities and workflow.**
- **Design system extraction.** During organization onboarding, a designated designer points Claude at a codebase (GitHub repo or local directory), existing design files, slide decks, or live website. Claude generates a reusable design system — color palette, typography scale, components (buttons, cards, navigation), and layout patterns — and publishes it, after which every project in that organization automatically inherits the brand. Teams can maintain multiple design systems and update them via a "Remix" interface.
- **Inputs.** Text prompts; uploads in DOCX, PPTX, XLSX, images, and screenshots; codebase links; and a **web capture tool** that grabs elements directly from a live URL so prototypes can mirror an existing product.
- **Output types Anthropic and customers cite:** interactive prototypes, product wireframes/mockups, design explorations, pitch decks/presentations, marketing collateral (landing pages, social, campaign visuals), microsites, and frontier code-powered prototypes with voice/video/3D/shaders/AI components.
- **Collaboration and sharing.** Organization-scoped — private, view-only, comment, or edit access; with edit access, multiple teammates can chat with Claude in the same conversation. Multiplayer real-time co-editing in the Figma sense is not supported.
- **Exports.** ZIP folder, PDF, PPTX, standalone HTML, internal shareable URL, send to **Canva** (where the design becomes fully editable inside Canva via Anthropic's existing partnership with Canva — confirmed by a quote from Canva CEO Melanie Perkins in the launch post), and the Claude Code handoff bundle. Notably, **there is no native Figma (.fig) export** at launch.

**Pricing and usage.** No additional cost on top of Pro ($20/mo), Max ($100–$200/mo), Team, or Enterprise. Per Anthropic's official pricing help article, Claude Design has its own recurring weekly allowance per provisioned user that resets every seven days, sitting alongside (not inside) chat and Claude Code limits. Subscription users can buy "extra usage" beyond the allowance at standard API pricing. Enterprise customers fall into legacy seat-based or usage-based billing, and Anthropic is offering a one-time **credit covering roughly 20 typical prompts per Claude Design user, expiring July 17, 2026**, consumed before any other Claude Design activity counts toward org spend. Anthropic explicitly notes the weekly allowances are "beta-period rate limits and are subject to change."

**Reviews and practitioner observations.**
- Anthropic's own launch testimonials: **Brilliant** (Senior Product Designer Olivia Xu) reported that pages needing 20+ prompts in other AI tools required only two in Claude Design, and that including design intent in the Claude Code handoff "made the jump from prototype to production seamless." **Datadog** (PM Aneesh Kethini) said it compressed week-long brief-mockup-review cycles into a single conversation. Canva CEO Melanie Perkins endorsed the integration.
- Hands-on developer reviews (DEV.to "launchwithben," Kay Zhang's wedding-site test, vibecoding.app, Builder.io, Muzli, Mejba, AiPromptsX, DataCamp explainer) are largely positive on three dimensions: design-system extraction from a real codebase produces tokens that match the source; first-pass prototypes are surprisingly close to brand voice when set up properly; and the Claude Code handoff genuinely works without a translation step. Builder.io and Kay Zhang both note edit-mode is more designer-oriented than typical AI coding tools because it shows context-appropriate controls (font options when a sentence is selected, layout options when a grid is selected) instead of a CSS dump.
- Common criticisms across practitioners and Hacker News: **weekly quota burn is the loudest complaint** — multiple users report 30 minutes of heavy use exhausting a Pro weekly allowance, two design sessions consuming 58–95% of weekly Pro limits (per @josuagolden, an HN thread "Thoughts and feelings around Claude Design," and PCWorld testing). Output is described by some HN commenters as "competent UI with little effort but nothing truly unique" — i.e., a strong baseline but mediocre without real design judgment layered on top. There is no native image generation (it draws SVGs and HTML, so photorealism requires swapping in third-party imagery); video output is weak relative to dedicated tools; there is no real-time multiplayer co-editing; and there is no public API at launch. Anthropic's own help docs flag known issues: **inline comments occasionally disappear before Claude reads them** (workaround: paste into chat); **save errors in compact view** (switch to full view); and **lag/browser issues with large monorepo links** (link subdirectories instead).
- Once a design is handed off to Claude Code, there is no path back into Claude Design — practitioners (e.g., Kay Zhang) note this means iteration after handoff happens in code, not on the design canvas. There is also no bidirectional sync with Figma or any native .fig export.

## Details

**The end-to-end workflow practitioners describe.** A user opens claude.ai/design, picks a role during onboarding (Design, Engineering, Product, Sales, Data, Marketing, Other), creates a project, and either describes what they want, uploads reference material (images, slide decks, PDFs, DOCX/PPTX/XLSX), points Claude at a code repo, or pastes a URL for the web-capture tool to ingest. Claude often asks 1–3 clarifying questions before generating a first draft on the canvas. From there, refinement uses chat for structural changes, inline comments on specific elements for local fixes, the Tweaks panel sliders for parameter tuning without burning chat tokens, and direct text edits for copy. When done, the Export menu offers ZIP, PDF, PPTX, Canva, standalone HTML, or "Handoff to Claude Code" (local agent or Claude Code Web).

**Anthropic's own framing of customer outcomes.** From the launch post:
- *Realistic prototypes* — turn static mockups into shareable interactive prototypes for user testing, no PRs needed.
- *Product wireframes and mockups* — PMs sketch feature flows for handoff to Claude Code or designers.
- *Design explorations* — designers run multiple directions in parallel.
- *Pitch decks and presentations* — rough outline to on-brand deck, exported PPTX or sent to Canva.
- *Marketing collateral* — landing pages, social assets, campaign visuals for designers to polish.
- *Frontier design* — code-powered prototypes with voice, video, shaders, 3D, and built-in AI.

**The X/Twitter launch post** (@claudeai, April 17, 2026): *"Introducing Claude Design by Anthropic Labs: make prototypes, slides, and one-pagers by talking to Claude. Powered by Claude Opus 4.7, our most capable vision model. Available in research preview on the Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise plans, rolling out throughout the day."* The Rundown AI's syndicated thread and numerous practitioner X threads (Austin Lau, Yair Geva, others) ran the same day.

**Anthropic Labs positioning.** Anthropic Labs is the experimental incubation team co-led by Mike Krieger and Ben Mann, formalized publicly in early 2026. Claude Code (which Anthropic says grew from research preview to a billion-dollar product in roughly six months), MCP, Skills, Claude in Chrome, and Cowork all came through the same model: ship a research preview, learn from real usage, then scale. Claude Design is the most ambitious application-layer Labs product to date, deliberately released the day after the model that powers it.

**Design system extraction in practice.** The "your brand, built in" claim is the feature most reviewers say differentiates Claude Design from generic AI design tools. The setup process: a design lead creates the organization, uploads brand and product assets (codebase, slide decks, style guide, logos, palette files), Claude generates a system covering color palette, typography scale, components, and layout patterns, the lead validates and edits, then publishes. Anthropic warns explicitly that without a published design system, output will be "functional but generic." Reviewer Ben (DEV.to) reported the Laravel-codebase brand-kit extraction produced a 12-step plan and took 30–40 minutes (~65% of which was context exploration), generating 26 preview cards plus a SKILL.md, with strong fidelity to the source CSS but thin icon coverage and some fabricated copy.

**Caveats and friction the launch coverage understates.** Several themes recur across hands-on writeups:
- *Token economics.* The interactive HTML output is several times richer than a typical chat turn. Multiple Pro users report exhausting a weekly allowance in a single session of heavy iteration. Anthropic's own help center confirms separate metering and acknowledges these are "beta-period rate limits, subject to change."
- *Production readiness.* Anthropic itself labels the product a research preview. Help docs list known bugs (vanishing inline comments, compact-view save errors, monorepo lag). Reviewer Mejba reported two rendering glitches (broken SVG paths on import, dark-mode palette not propagating to nested components) in a hands-on session.
- *Iteration after Claude Code handoff is one-way.* Once a design moves to Claude Code, edits happen in code; there is no bidirectional sync back into the design canvas (Kay Zhang's review). Practitioners flag this as the obvious next-version need.
- *Not a replacement for senior design judgment.* Multiple reviewers (Craftly, Mejba, ALM Corp, Builder.io) emphasize that Claude Design raises the floor for non-designers but does not close the gap between "decent" and "considered, intentional, well-crafted" work.

**Pricing detail per Anthropic's pricing help article.** Pro/Max users get a recurring weekly allowance with extra usage available at API rates. Team/Enterprise users get per-user allowances (not pooled at org level). Enterprise legacy seat-based and usage-based plans differ in how Claude Design is billed; usage-based Enterprise users get the one-time ~20-prompt credit expiring July 17. Opus 4.7 list pricing remained $5/M input and $25/M output, but the new tokenizer's 1×–1.35× token consumption on the same text materially affects effective cost (per Anthropic's own Opus 4.7 release notes and third-party reviews from Verdent, Findskill, Fello AI, Evolink, and The AI Corner).

## Caveats

- **Claude Design is a research preview**, and Anthropic's own help docs say the experience may change. Limits, capabilities, integrations, and even pricing buckets are explicitly subject to change. Treat any specific number (Brilliant's "20+ → 2," Datadog's "week → one conversation," weekly quota figures) as illustrative rather than universal — they are vendor-supplied or single-user reports.
- **Some practitioner figures are anecdotal.** The "Figma stock dropped 7%" claim is well-documented in finance press; the "two prompts ate 95% of my weekly allowance" type claims come from individual X and Reddit users (e.g., @josuagolden) and are not systematic measurements. Token cost dynamics depend heavily on prompt patterns, design system setup quality, and whether you use chat versus the Tweaks panel.
- **The Mike Krieger / Figma narrative is partially circumstantial.** The SEC filing and timing are real and verified; the framing of the launch as a Figma assault is largely media interpretation. Anthropic publicly insists Claude Design complements rather than replaces existing tools, while shipping a feature set that overtly competes with Figma's first-draft and prototyping flows. Take both framings into account.
- **Several details may date quickly.** Anthropic announced "in the coming weeks" expansions for integrations, an API surface, and additional export formats. The "Claude for Creative Work" announcement on April 28, 2026 already extended the surrounding ecosystem with new connectors. Some specifics in this report (e.g., no Figma export, no public API, no real-time multiplayer) reflect launch-window status and are likely to evolve.
- **No independent benchmarking exists yet.** Comparisons between Claude Design and Figma Make, v0, Lovable, Bolt, Google Stitch, or GPT Image 2.0 in the coverage are based on hands-on impressions rather than standardized evaluations. As of the most recent dates in the source material, Anthropic itself describes the design system extraction as best with clean source assets and acknowledges that messy inputs produce messy systems.
- I'm summarizing publicly available reporting and reviews; I'm not an Anthropic employee and have not independently tested Claude Design. For decisions that depend on exact pricing, quotas, or enterprise availability, verify against Anthropic's live help center, your account dashboard, and your admin team.
