The Three Pillars — Not Participation Reasons. Governance Boundaries.
Guardrail One
Trust
Client Boundary
The floor the client stands on
Every action inside the system is taken in the client's interest. This is not a soft promise — it is an architectural constraint. The system cannot present a match that serves the institution at the client's expense. Governed disclosure. Depth by development. Never extractive.
Guardrail Two
Assurance
Institutional Boundary
The wall the institution operates inside
Every institution enters a scenario with complete confidence the data is accurate, the analysis is governed, and their exposure is bounded by contract. Bilateral risk reading happens before any institution sees a client scenario. Their NIL — name, image, likeness — is never at risk.
Guardrail Three
Reinsurance
System Boundary
The ceiling that holds when outcomes shift
When circumstances change after deployment — market moves, client conditions shift, product terms change — the reinsurance layer is the governed backstop. The operation stays coherent and auditable even when the outcome diverges from the plan. The system absorbs the drift without breaking.
