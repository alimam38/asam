A Message from the Presiding Elder

The district continues to stand as a testament to the faithfulness of God — and to twenty-four congregations who carry the mission, week after week.

It is with deep gratitude and a full heart that I present this report to the 152nd Session of the Atlanta North Georgia Annual Conference. The South Atlanta District continues to stand as a testament to the faithfulness of God and the dedication of twenty-four congregations who, week after week, carry the mission of the African Methodist Episcopal Church into the communities they serve.

This past conference year has been marked by extraordinary growth and renewed commitment. Across the district, our churches have welcomed one hundred fifty-eight conversions — men and women who have answered the call to discipleship. Fifty-one were baptized. One hundred ninety-three new members were received through accession.

Even in the shadow of loss, with thirty souls departed from our earthly fellowship, the body of Christ in this district has grown stronger.

"The Best Is Yet to Come — not as an aspiration, but as a conviction. The work before us is great, but so is the God who has called us to it."

Rev. Dr. Larry W. Hudson, Sr. · Presiding Elder

The financial stewardship of this district is a story worth telling with pride. Our twenty-four congregations raised a combined $5,589,314 locally — a figure that reflects not only the generosity of our membership, but the trust the people of this district place in the mission of the Church. From the smallest charge to our largest congregation, every dollar raised represents a family that believed, a ministry that gave, and a community that continued to invest in the work of the Kingdom.

The South Atlanta District enters the year ahead from a position of momentum. The growth recorded in this report — in conversions, in stewardship, in civic engagement — is not accidental. It is the fruit of congregations that have committed to their communities, to their calling, and to one another.

What God has done in this district in this conference year is a foundation — not a ceiling.
