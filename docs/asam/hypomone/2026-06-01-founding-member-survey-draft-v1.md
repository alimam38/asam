# Founding Member Survey — Draft v1

**Working title for the instrument:** *The Membership Study*
**Purpose (internal note — not shown to respondents):** Validate the customer base hypothesis, surface what membership should mean, convert respondents into founding stakeholders, and produce citable first-party data. Built for two overlapping segments: real estate investors and self-employed / business-owning operators who experience structural misbanking.

---

## Design notes before the questions

**Length target:** 5–7 minutes. Sophisticated operators abandon long surveys. Every question earns its place or it's cut.

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, not institution-to-applicant. The language assumes the respondent is a capable operator, never a supplicant. No condescension, no "fam" familiarity, no banking jargon that signals the old system.

**Structure:** Five short sections. Screening → Experience → Behavior → Membership → Stake. The first four validate and gather; the fifth converts.

**Critical methodology point:** This instrument generates first-party data you own. Unlike the secondary-source landscape scan, responses here are citable primary evidence — provided the instrument is administered cleanly (consistent wording, documented sample, no leading questions). Where a question is designed to produce a citable statistic, it's marked [DATA].

**A caution built into the design:** The survey must not promise specific financial products, rates, or approval. Doing so could create regulatory exposure (implied offer of credit) before licensing and structure are in place. Language throughout stays at the level of interest and experience, not offer. Marked [COMPLIANCE] where it matters.

## Appendix — What this instrument produces (internal)

**For the Keena update:** Clean, first-party, citable data on (a) the prevalence of misbanking experiences in the segment, (b) the financial volume and product usage the segment represents, (c) the payment reliability of the segment, and (d) demonstrated demand for a membership institution. This is the evidence base that replaces the secondary-source scan.

**For the build:** A prioritized list of what membership should mean (Q13), the objections to design around (Q15), the price posture (Q14), and the founding community itself (Q16–Q19).

**For the institutional sales story:** Q8 + Q9 + Q10 + Q11 together produce the line you bring to banks — "here is a segment moving [X] in volume, using [Y] products, paying [Z] reliably, and the institutions serving them don't recognize the value of the relationship."

**Methodology to preserve citability:**
- Document the sample: how many invited, how many responded, response rate, how respondents were sourced (this matters — a survey of Keena's network is not a random sample, and the writeup must say so honestly).
- Don't change question wording mid-field.
- Report ranges and distributions, not cherry-picked figures.
- Distinguish clearly between what the data shows (demand signal from a specific network) and what it doesn't show (representativeness of the broader U.S. market). Honesty about the sample's limits is what makes the credible parts credible.

## Open questions for you before v2

1. **Instrument name.** "The Membership Study" is a placeholder. The name sets the tone — worth choosing deliberately.
2. **Two segments, one instrument or two?** This draft handles both in one with branching. If the real estate investor segment and the operator segment turn out to need meaningfully different questions, we split. My read: keep it unified for now; the overlap justifies it and a single instrument is easier to administer and analyze.
3. **Platform.** Where does this live — Typeform, Tally, a custom build? The platform affects the experience and the data quality. Worth deciding before launch.
4. **Keena's hand in it.** She offered to co-design and champion. Which questions does she shape, and does her name or Prep Capital's appear as a co-author of the study? That changes how respondents in her network receive it.
