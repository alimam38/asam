# Nonprofit/Education Pricing Scout — 2026-07-13 (first run)

First-ever run of this scout. Eligible entity: Turner Theological Seminary (501(c)(3), accredited
seminary). All terms below were verified via web search/fetch on 2026-07-13; anything not pinned
down is flagged "verify current terms."

**Cross-cutting finding (matters for everything below):** Turner sits in BOTH lanes — 501(c)(3)
nonprofit AND accredited educational institution — and several "for Nonprofits" programs
explicitly **exclude educational institutions** (Google for Nonprofits, Canva, AWS credits, Zoom
nonprofit, GitHub nonprofit is "non-academic"). Whether validators (Goodstack, TechSoup) classify
Turner as "nonprofit" or "school" will decide which lane each program lands in. Practical play:
apply on the 501(c)(3) religious-organization identity where possible, and use the education track
(free Google Workspace for Education, edu hardware pricing) as the fallback — it's often just as
good. Getting **TechSoup validation + a Goodstack verification done once** unlocks most of the list.

## Programs

### 1. Google Workspace — free (two tracks: Nonprofits or Education Fundamentals)
- **What you get:** Gmail on your domain, Drive, Meet, Docs/Sheets, Gemini/NotebookLM — free via
  Google for Nonprofits; or Google Workspace for Education Fundamentals, free to accredited
  institutions (this is the safer lane for a seminary, since Google for Nonprofits generally
  excludes academic institutions).
- **Estimated value:** ~$6–18/user/month vs. paid Workspace (~$1,500–4,500/yr for a 20-person org).
- **Validation:** Nonprofits track → Goodstack verification (3–5 business days; watch for email
  from verifications@mail.goodstack.org). Education track → domain verification + scanned
  accreditation document; ~14 business days.
- **Next action:** Apply for Education Fundamentals with Turner's accreditation letter; try the
  Nonprofits track in parallel and keep whichever approves.
- **Source:** https://www.google.com/nonprofits/eligibility/ ·
  https://knowledge.workspace.google.com/admin/getting-started/editions/qualifications-for-google-workspace-for-education

### 2. Microsoft for Nonprofits — M365 grants + $2,000/yr Azure credits
- **What you get:** Up to 300 granted (free) Microsoft 365 Business Basic licenses; up to 75% off
  paid M365 SKUs (Business Premium ~$5.50/user/mo vs. $22 commercial); **$2,000/yr Azure credit
  grant** (usable for VMs, databases, hosting — relevant to Plumbline). Note: the old free
  Business Premium grant was discontinued; Basic is the granted tier now. Credits/licenses don't
  roll over year to year.
- **Estimated value:** Basic grant alone worth up to ~$21k/yr at 300 seats (realistically
  $1–2k/yr at seminary scale) + $2,000/yr Azure.
- **Validation:** Microsoft nonprofit eligibility verification (501(c)(3)); register at the
  Microsoft Nonprofits portal.
- **Next action:** Register Turner at microsoft.com/nonprofits, claim Business Basic grant and
  activate the Azure grant.
- **Source:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/nonprofits/faq ·
  https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/industry/nonprofit/microsoft-for-nonprofits/claim-activate-nonprofit-azure-grant

### 3. AWS Nonprofit Credit Program — $1,000/yr promotional credits (via TechSoup)
- **What you get:** $1,000 USD AWS promotional credit per fiscal year (Jul 1–Jun 30), valid ≥1
  year, usable on all on-demand pay-as-you-go services — direct Plumbline hosting money.
- **Estimated value:** $1,000/yr.
- **Validation:** TechSoup registration/validation; org budget must be under $10M.
  **Caveat to verify:** educational institutions (colleges, universities, trade schools) are
  explicitly NOT eligible — whether an accredited seminary registered as a 501(c)(3) religious
  org passes depends on TechSoup's classification. If rejected, AWS Educate / education programs
  are the fallback.
- **Next action:** Complete TechSoup validation first (see #6), then request the credit grant at
  page.techsoup.org/aws before the fiscal year gets consumed.
- **Source:** https://aws.amazon.com/government-education/nonprofits/nonprofit-credit-program/ ·
  https://www.techsoup.org/support/articles-and-how-tos/aws-nonprofit-credit-program-faq

### 4. Atlassian — Teamwork Collection 100% free for nonprofits (new Feb 2026)
- **What you get:** Jira + Confluence + Loom (Teamwork Collection, Standard or Premium) at 100%
  off for up to 25 users — expanded in Feb 2026 from the old 75% discount. Marketplace apps 75%
  off. Requires annual billing cycle, ≤25 users.
- **Estimated value:** roughly $3–5k/yr for a small team at Standard tier.
- **Validation:** Atlassian Community/nonprofit eligibility review (501(c)(3) proof).
- **Next action:** Apply at atlassian.com/teams/nonprofits, then add Teamwork Collection on
  annual billing to trigger the 100% discount.
- **Source:** https://www.atlassian.com/blog/announcements/new-nonprofit-discounts ·
  https://www.atlassian.com/teams/nonprofits/discount-pricing

### 5. GitHub for Nonprofits — free Team plan, unlimited users/private repos
- **What you get:** GitHub Team plan free (normally $4/user/mo): unlimited private repos,
  unlimited users — the natural home for Plumbline's repos.
- **Estimated value:** ~$48/user/yr; a few hundred $/yr at small-team scale, scales with team.
- **Validation:** Apply through the GitHub for Nonprofits portal as an org owner; auto-validates
  if already in their nonprofit database, otherwise a validation application (~1 week).
  **Caveat:** eligibility wording is "non-governmental, non-academic, non-commercial,
  non-political" — apply under the 501(c)(3) religious-organization identity; verify how they
  treat a seminary.
- **Next action:** From the Plumbline GitHub org (owner account), apply at
  github.com/solutions/industry/nonprofits.
- **Source:** https://docs.github.com/en/nonprofit/nonprofit-teams-plan/getting-started-with-the-github-team-plan-for-nonprofits

### 6. TechSoup — the validation gateway (register once, unlocks many programs)
- **What you get:** Free membership; donated/discounted software and refurb hardware at 92–96%
  below retail (Microsoft, Adobe, Intuit, Cisco...), each with a $5–125 admin fee. More
  importantly: **validation tokens** (format `123a4567@OrgName`, 60-day life, renewable, created
  by the org's authorized agent ~30 min after validation) that other vendors (e.g. AWS) accept
  as proof of nonprofit status.
- **Estimated value:** enabler for #3 and others; direct product savings 92–96% off retail.
- **Validation:** Register org at techsoup.org with 501(c)(3) determination letter; TechSoup
  validates, then authorized agent can mint tokens.
- **Next action:** Register Turner on techsoup.org and get validated — this is the single
  highest-leverage 30 minutes on this list.
- **Source:** https://www.techsoup.org/support/validation-tokens ·
  https://www.techsoup.global/frequently-asked-questions

### 7. Canva for Nonprofits — Pro + Teams free for up to 50 users
- **What you get:** All Canva Pro features + team collaboration for one team of up to 50 users,
  free, no time limit while eligible; extra seats 50% off.
- **Estimated value:** ~$120/user/yr → up to ~$6k/yr at 50 seats.
- **Validation:** Goodstack (same partner as Google/Zoom/Adobe); IRS determination letter;
  decision within ~7 days. **Caveat:** educational institutions are on Canva's ineligible list —
  apply as the 501(c)(3) charity, not as a school; verify current terms for seminaries.
- **Next action:** Apply at canva.com/nonprofits with the IRS determination letter.
- **Source:** https://www.canva.com/nonprofits/eligibility-guidelines/

### 8. Adobe — Express free; Acrobat Pro ~$15/yr; Creative Cloud via TechSoup
- **What you get:** Adobe Express for Nonprofits free (direct from Adobe); Acrobat Pro nonprofit
  pricing ~$15/yr individual (vs. ~$240/yr retail — note: as of 2026 Acrobat Pro is no longer on
  TechSoup, it's direct via Adobe); Creative Cloud still via TechSoup, now in unlimited
  quantities.
- **Estimated value:** ~$225/yr saved per Acrobat seat; Express free vs. ~$120/yr.
- **Validation:** Goodstack (Adobe's verification partner as of 2026) for direct offers;
  TechSoup for Creative Cloud.
- **Next action:** Request Express (free) + Acrobat nonprofit pricing at adobe.com/nonprofits;
  get Creative Cloud through TechSoup once validated.
- **Source:** https://helpx.adobe.com/enterprise/using/non-profit.html ·
  https://www.techsoup.org/adobe

### 9. Slack for Nonprofits — free Pro plan (workspaces ≤250 members)
- **What you get:** Free upgrade to Slack Pro for one workspace with ≤250 members (85% off
  Pro/Business+ above 250).
- **Estimated value:** ~$105/user/yr (Pro list ~$8.75/user/mo) — ~$2k/yr for a 20-person
  workspace.
- **Validation:** Application via Slack's nonprofit portal; charitable status verified via
  TechSoup (and Goodstack/Percent in some regions).
- **Next action:** Apply at slack.com/nonprofit for the existing (or a new) Turner/ASAM
  workspace.
- **Source:** https://slack.com/help/articles/204368833-Apply-for-the-Slack-for-Nonprofits-discount

### 10. Zoom for Nonprofits — 50% off select products (now direct via Goodstack)
- **What you get:** 50% off annual Zoom Workplace Pro, Workplace Business, Large Meeting add-on,
  and Zoom Webinar. Note: the TechSoup–Zoom partnership ended May 2025 — apply direct through
  Zoom Cares with Goodstack verification.
- **Estimated value:** ~$6.50–8/user/mo saved on Workplace Pro.
- **Validation:** Goodstack; 501(c)(3); operating budget ≤$10M. **Caveat:** K-12/colleges are
  routed to Zoom for Education (per-institution quotes) instead of the nonprofit discount —
  same nonprofit-vs-school classification question as above.
- **Next action:** Apply via zoom.com/en/zoom-cares (Goodstack flow) before renewing any paid
  Zoom plan at list price.
- **Source:** https://support.zoom.com/hc/en/article?id=zm_kb&sysparm_article=KB0080298 ·
  https://support.techsoup.org/hc/en-us/articles/36503175618203-Zoom-for-Nonprofits-No-Longer-Available-on-TechSoup
