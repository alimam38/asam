# The Charter — v2

**Working title for the instrument:** *The Charter*
**Institution it enrolls members into:** Hypomone (the membership institution Meridia is standing up)
**Internal purpose (not shown to respondents):** Validate the customer-base hypothesis against the federally-documented misbanked segment, surface what membership should mean, convert respondents into charter members of the founding cohort, and produce citable first-party institutional data that the institution owns from day one.

---

## Design notes before the questions

**Length target:** 5–7 minutes. Sophisticated operators abandon long surveys. Every question earns its place or it's cut.

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer, not institution-to-applicant. The respondent is a capable operator the system has misread, not a supplicant the institution will rescue. No condescension, no "fam" familiarity, no banking jargon that signals the old system, and — equally — no over-reverent ritual language that would feel performative at first contact. The rite is the *signing*, not the questionnaire.

**Structure:** Five short sections. **Recognition → Experience → Behavior → Membership → Charter.** The first four read and gather; the fifth converts respondents into charter members of The Solera Principle.

**Critical methodology point:** This instrument generates first-party data the institution owns. Unlike a secondary-source scan, responses here are citable primary evidence — *provided the instrument is administered cleanly* (consistent wording, documented sample, no leading questions). Where a question is designed to produce a citable statistic, it's marked [DATA].

**Compliance built into the design:** The Charter must not promise specific financial products, rates, or approval. Doing so would create regulatory exposure (implied offer of credit) before licensing and structure are in place — and given the founder's NMLS standing, the bar here is non-negotiable. Language throughout stays at the level of interest and experience, never offer. Marked [COMPLIANCE] where it matters.

**On the segment definition:** This draft addresses what the federal data names the **misbanked** — capable, financially-active operators (self-employed, investors, business owners, non-traditional earners) whose income or structure conventional underwriting misreads. *Not* unbanked or underbanked (FDIC's terms, different populations). The misbanking distinction matters; the survey should let respondents see themselves in it without having to name it that way.

## Appendix — What this instrument produces (internal)

**For the Keena package and future capital conversations:** Clean, first-party, citable data on (a) the prevalence of misbanking experiences in the cohort, (b) the financial volume and product usage the segment represents, (c) the payment reliability of the segment, (d) demonstrated demand for the institution, and (e) — newly — whether the Atlanta Fed's behavioral signal (credit cards as working capital) reproduces in the founding sample. The federal data establishes the segment exists at national and metro scale; The Charter establishes it exists *in this room*, with names attached.

**For the build:** A prioritized list of what membership should mean (Q13), the objections to design around (Q15), the price posture (Q14), and the founding cohort itself (Q16–Q19).

**For the institutional sales story** (Hypomone Financial Network Partners): Q8 + Q9 + Q10 + Q11 together produce the line that goes to banks — *"here is a segment moving [X] in volume, using [Y] products, paying [Z] reliably, and the institutions serving them don't recognize the value of the relationship."*

**Methodology to preserve citability:**
- Document the sample honestly: how many invited, how many responded, response rate, how respondents were sourced. A survey administered through one network is *not* a random sample, and the writeup must say so directly. Honest sample disclosure is what makes the credible parts credible.
- Do not change question wording mid-field.
- Report ranges and distributions, not cherry-picked figures.
- Distinguish clearly between what the data shows (demand signal from a specific founding network) and what it does not show (representativeness of the broader U.S. misbanked population). The federal data carries the national claim; The Charter carries the cohort claim. Keep them separate.
